Frequent Asked Questions for Children

How do I know what therapy is right for my child?

We will use our assessment to guide you about what therapy would be most helpful for your child and family.
What should I do if I am worried about mine or my child’s risk?

If you are concerned about yours or your child’s risk contact 111 or 999. Please do not contact your therapist as we do not offer an emergency service. If your child is known to CAMHS you can call the crisis team.
How many therapy sessions will my child or family need?

It is difficult to say how much therapy you or your child may need as it will depend on what you are seeking support for. We can think about this with you in the assessment sessions and plan therapy around your goals and budget.
Will my child’s sessions be confidential?

You and your child have the right to a confidential relationship, within certain legal limits. Under certain legally defined situations, we have a duty under penalty of law and duty of care to reveal information to other persons without your written consent. These situations include: If you reveal information that indicates you or someone else, or your child, are at risk of harm or abuse. If your child discloses abuse. If your child is in therapy or being tested due to an order of a court or lawyer, the results of the treatment ordered must be revealed to that court or lawyer. If a court of law issues a legitimate order, we are required by law to provide the information specifically described in that court order. With the exception of the situations defined above information revealed by you during the course of therapy will be kept completely confidential. Any records will include only information relevant for current therapeutic purposes. Any information will be kept securely and in line with guidance on storing medical notes.
Can my Childs therapist talk to other professionals involved in my child’s care?

If you give permission your child’s therapist can talk to other professionals involved in your child’s care. Please be aware this will not be done without your written consent expect within the context of there being concerns about your child’s safely or child protection, in which case this can be done without your consent.
What if I need to cancel my appointment?

Appointments can be cancelled once booked but unfortunately if you give less than 24 hours notice the appointment will need to be paid for in full. This is because we cannot offer this to another to someone else.
How do I pay for appointments?

Appointment are invoiced when they booked and payment is due 48 hours prior to the appointment by direct bank transfer. Without payment the appointment will be cancelled. You are welcome to rebook.
What are the costs of therapy?

Cost vary depending on the service, so please get in touch and we can advise you based on your needs.
Do you work with insurance and private health care companies?

At Ebb and Flow we are registered with a number of insurance companies so please get in touch if you have private health care cover.
