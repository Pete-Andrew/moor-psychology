About Us


At Ebb and Flow Psychology we appreciate that our lives can be complex as we juggle multiple demands and the ebb and flow of our daily lives. At times this can be difficult for us all and can impact on our mental health. We recognise that our emotional worlds can be like the ocean tides, coming and going, sometimes calm and sometimes stormy, pulled by forces often not known to our conscious minds. We appreciate that we may all need time to pause, reflect, process our emotions and take care of ourselves. All too often, we can neglect our own well-being and struggle to give ourselves the care and compassion we need. We offer you the opportunity to slow down and explore your experiences and emotions in a safe place, free of judgment, supporting you to be with the ebb and flow of the tides of your life.

We recognise the importance of supporting individuals and families to process experiences and traumas which may be impacting on their relationships and lives in the present. We know that trauma may show up in all shapes and sizes and can impact on our lives in varied and individual ways.

At Ebb and Flow Psychology we specialise in caring for the whole family, recognising the importance of caring for parents in order to help you care for your children. We understand that children and adults experience things differently and support parents to deepen their understanding of their child’s world and strengthen their relationships. We believe it’s important to start early and offer support through pregnancy and to parents and their babies. We know that supporting our children to face challenges and being with the emotions this brings helps strengthen families and build resilience into the future. We recognise that family relationships can be challenging and support all to feel more connected. With many years of experience supporting children and their families we are confident in engaging children of all ages in playful and creative ways.

What you Can Expect


As Clinical Psychologists we have specialist knowledge and training in mental health, so you can feel confident we have the skills to support you. We know everyone is individual and work with you around what is important to you and your family. We know what happens to us can have a profound impact on our lives and seek to support our clients to explore their experiences in a safe, creative and compassionate space. At Ebb and Flow we see our role as being alongside you on your therapeutic journey. We work at your pace, helping you to gain a greater understanding of your life, emotions and experiences. We support you to connect to those things that anchor you to safety and are precious in your life, supporting you to feel more at ease and empowered.

What is a Clinical Psychologist


Clinical psychologists are trained to work with children, young people and adults experiencing difficulties in their emotional well-being, mental or physical health or disability. We work to help reduce psychological distress and promote psychological well-being. As Clinical psychologists we use our knowledge of psychological theory to help us understand the difficulties you and your child or family may be experiencing and work with you to help bring about change.

Clinical psychologists train for over 6 years and have an undergraduate degree as well as a post graduate doctorate in Clinical Psychology. Clinical psychologists are registered with the Health Care Professions Council HCPC so you can be confident we are highly trained and regulated. This is really important as anyone can call themselves a Psychologist, which can be confusing.
